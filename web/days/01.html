Training AI to be my video editor

written · edited · captioned · published — by AI

A random account reposted my video on Instagram and got more comments than my original got likes. So I'm spending 30 days teaching an AI to do the work for me. Day 1 is the planning episode — and the plugin you can install to do this for your own series.

Install the Day 1 plugin

Two lines into Claude Code (free at claude.com/code):

$ /plugin marketplace add andrewjiang/andrewislearning
$ /plugin install day-01-series-planner@andrewislearning

Then ask Claude: "Help me plan a 30-day series about [your topic]." The skill walks you through five questions and writes SERIES_PLAN.md, DAY_01_SCRIPT.md, and TOOLS.md straight into your project folder.

How this video was made

The selfie was shot on a phone. Everything else — the cuts, the captions, the b-roll, the audio polish, the publish — was done by code that calls AI agents. Six pipeline scripts running in sequence, every one open-source.

transcribe.py — Whisper

Word-level timestamps from raw selfie footage. Local, no API.

tighten.py — keep-windows

Cuts dead air and long pauses based on a JSON keep list. Removed 5+ seconds of "ums" and gaps.

assemble.py — b-roll inserter

Selfie audio is the spine. Three b-roll clips replace the visual at scripted moments while audio continues.

caption.py — burned-in captions

Reads the transcript, groups phrases (so "AI agent" stays atomic), generates ASS subtitles, ffmpeg burns them in.

finish.py — audio + color + speed

Loudness normalize to -14 LUFS (IG/TikTok target), light color grade, 1.08× tempo. One ffmpeg pass.

publish.py — Post for Me API

Uploads to Post for Me, publishes to Instagram Reels + TikTok in one API call. Receipt saved with permalinks.

What the plugin actually does

The series-planner skill bottles the conversation that planned this very series. It asks five questions:

  • What are you learning to do over 30 days, and what rules constrain it?
  • Where will it be published, and who's the target viewer?
  • What's the inciting incident — the specific story that made you start?
  • What one credential do you bring that serves the story?
  • What's the brand handle?

Then it builds a 4-week arc, drafts the Day 1 script, and writes the tool rollout. The Day 1 script template includes the structure that tested well in this series: format-opener, inciting incident with specific numbers, ownership flip, rules, meta moment, card stack, comment-to-DM CTA.
