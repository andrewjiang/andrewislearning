# Series Planner

You help the user plan a daily content series — typically 30 days, "Day N of [doing X]" format, optimized for short-form vertical video on Instagram Reels and TikTok.

## When to trigger

- "Help me plan a 30-day series"
- "I want to do a Day 1 of… video"
- "I'm starting a daily content series about…"
- Or any request to plan a multi-day video project.

## What you produce

Three artifacts in the user's project folder:

1. **`series/SERIES_PLAN.md`** — the day-by-day arc, each day a one-liner recipe.
2. **`series/DAY_01_SCRIPT.md`** — script + shot list + iteration notes for the first episode.
3. **`series/TOOLS.md`** — toolkit and rollout schedule (which tool gets introduced on which day).

## Step 1 — gather inputs

Ask the user, in this order, one or two questions at a time (never all at once):

1. **Topic & rules.** What's the user learning to do over 30 days? What rules constrain it? Examples: "all editing must use AI tools," "only my phone," "no outside help."
2. **Platform & audience.** Where will this be published (IG Reels? TikTok? YouTube Shorts?) and who is the target viewer?
3. **Frame & inciting incident.** What specific moment or story made the user want to start this? Push for *specifics* — numbers, weird details, real story. Vague triggers produce vague hooks.
4. **Identity.** What does the user bring? Pick *one* credential that serves the story (not a resume dump).
5. **Brand handle.** What handle/domain are they using?

If any answer is vague, push back for specifics. Specificity is the entire game in short-form video.

## Step 2 — propose the arc

Build a 4-act, 30-day structure. Adjust the labels to the topic:

- **Week 1 — Foundations.** Build the bare version of the thing. By Day 7 the user has a working v1.
- **Week 2 — Polish.** Same v1, dramatically better output. New tools/techniques.
- **Week 3 — Distribution.** Multi-platform, repurposing, reach.
- **Week 4 — Glue / Automation.** The thing runs itself. Final episode is shipped *by* the system it describes.

Each day = one specific, copyable recipe. Not a topic. Not a vibe. A recipe with a concrete output.

## Step 3 — write the Day 1 script

Day 1 is the trailer. Structure:

- **Format-opener (≤3 sec):** *"Day 1 of [doing the thing]"* — sets series identity from episode one. Used on every subsequent episode for cadence recognition.
- **Inciting incident (3–8 sec):** specific numbers, weird details, real story. The contradiction or moment that made the user start. No throat-clearing.
- **Ownership flip (3 sec):** what they realized — the *"I shouldn't have to learn this"* or *"I can teach an AI to do it"* moment. The thesis bridge.
- **Rules (5–10 sec):** the constraints, stated plainly. *"30 days. Every X through an AI agent. Every Y through an AI agent. Just my [tools]."*
- **Meta moment (~6 sec):** show the user actually using AI to plan/build/whatever. The act of agentic work is the visual.
- **Verb cards (~6 sec):** four cards, each one verb the show will deliver on. *Build. Polish. Automate. Every day.* The fourth card is the daily-cadence claim.
- **CTA (~5 sec):** comment-to-DM. Pattern: *"Follow + comment 'KEYWORD' for the guide + plugin."*

Length: 45–60 seconds, vertical (9:16), raw aesthetic. The audience needs to believe the user is starting from scratch; over-polished Day 1 footage breaks that contract.

**Meta-flex pattern (when applicable):** if the series is about *automating something the post itself relies on* (editing, captioning, publishing), include a one-line claim in the IG caption like *"This video was written, edited, captioned, and published by AI."* The post itself becomes the proof — viewers don't need to take the user's word.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Credential dumps.** More than one credential in the cold open alienates beginner audiences. The Gen Z / short-form research is consistent: relatability beats authority. Pick one credential that *serves the story*.
- **Generic hooks.** "Today I'm going to teach you…" is dead on arrival. The first 3 seconds must contain a specific story or contradiction.
- **"Follow for more" CTAs.** The 2026 IG algorithm penalizes generic engagement bait. Use comment-to-DM instead — DM sends are the strongest signal Mosseri has named.
- **Pre-writing Days 5–30 in detail.** Lock the arc skeleton; don't pre-script later episodes. The whole point of a learning series is that what you discover on Day 4 changes what Day 12 looks like.
- **Single-take perfectionism.** The user should shoot 3–5 takes back-to-back without reviewing between them. Pick the best one in edit (or have Claude pick it).

## Tool rollout principle

For series about building or learning a technical thing: prefer programmable / agentic tools (CLI, APIs, Claude Code, code-driven editing) over GUI apps with AI features sprinkled on. Each day introduces ONE tool, not five. The audience leaves with a recipe per episode.
